About

I am a PhD candidate, advised by Prof. Alex Snoeren, in the Systems and Networking group at the UC San Diego Computer Science and Engineering department. My research is focused on enabling user-defined behavior on programmable switches using active networking [1,2].

While at UCSD, I have also worked on datacenter fault detection [4] and the RotorNet project [3]. Prior to joining UCSD, I was a part of the PinDrop project at Microsoft Research India where I worked on improving quality of service of real-time streaming applications [5,6]. During my Masters program at IIT Kanpur, I worked on building educational technologies for introductory programming courses [thesis].

Current Research

As a part of my ongoing work, I am building a system that enables user-defined behavior to be deployed within a programmable network by means of active networking. Programs encoded as code and data are embedded in network packets and subsequently decoded and executed on a programmable switch. Virtualized memory is dynamically split among applications and isolated at runtime [1]. This research has also been supported by the Intel FFI program.
